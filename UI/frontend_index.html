What Can a Consumer Do If a Problem Occurs?

The Consumer Protection Act, 2019 empowers consumers with specific rights, remedies, and legal mechanisms to address problems such as defective products, poor services, refund denial, online fraud, and unfair trade practices. Below is a complete overview of the legal options available to a consumer.

Common Consumer Problems

Defective / Faulty Product

Manufacturing defects, damaged goods

Refund or Return Denied

Seller refusing refund or replacement

Online Fraud or Scam

Cheated by online seller or platform

Poor or Delayed Service

Service not provided or delayed

Misleading Advertisement

False claims, fake discounts

E-Commerce Issues

Platform accountability issues

Consumer Rights Under the Act

Right to Safety

Protection against hazardous goods and services.

Right to Information

Correct information about quality, price, and risks.

Right to Choice

Freedom to choose goods and services.

Right to Be Heard

Consumer complaints must be considered.

Right to Seek Redressal

Refund, replacement, compensation.

Right to Consumer Education

Awareness of rights and remedies.

Steps to Take If a Consumer Problem Occurs

  1. Identify the consumer problem
  2. Understand the applicable consumer right
  3. Seek remedy from seller or service provider
  4. Collect proof (invoice, communication, etc.)
  5. File a complaint before the Consumer Commission
  6. Seek refund, compensation, or corrective action
